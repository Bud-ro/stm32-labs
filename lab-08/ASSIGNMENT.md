## Assignment

This lab drives the class-board DC motor (DG01D-E through an L298N
H-bridge) with timer PWM, reads the motor's single-channel encoder via
EXTI rising-edge interrupts, and reports RPM over the ST-Link VCP.

Pin map per the shield schematic:

- `MOTOR_PWM` → PA4, TIM14_CH1 (AF4). The driver runs the timer at
  10 kHz with a 100-tick period, so `DUTY50` writes 50 into `CCR1`
  and the L298N IN1 sees a 50 % duty cycle.
- `ENCODER` → PA0, EXTI line 0. Configured for rising edge; the ISR
  just clears the pending flag and bumps a `u32` pulse counter.

The application keeps the structure from prior labs: a UART parser
backed by the shared `CommandBuffer`, plus a 1 Hz `erd_core` timer
that snapshots the encoder count and prints `RPM` together with the
raw pulses-per-second so you can sanity-check the gearbox constant.
Commands:

- `DUTY<n>` for `n` ∈ 0..100 - set PWM duty cycle. Below ~30 % the
  motor will stall; the firmware still accepts the command.
- `HALT` - force duty to zero. RPM continues to report (will read
  zero once the rotor coasts to a stop).

The RPM math is `pulses_per_second * 60 / ENCODER_PULSES_PER_REV`.
`ENCODER_PULSES_PER_REV` is a constant in `application.zig`; for the
DG01D-E it's 144 (3 rising edges per motor revolution from the
6-pole Hall magnet, times the 1:48 gearbox). Adjust if your motor
differs.

The 9 V motor supply must share ground with the Nucleo's 3.3 V
ground (per the note on the shield schematic).

The original assignment was this:

Project

Timers are one of the most complex peripherals on a modern
microcontroller. They are often used in conjunction with or without
interrupts. In this week's coding assignment, let's explore how to
use it. The goal is to drive a DC motor to certain speed and read
back its speed feedback. The PWM signal is generated by a timer
module, and the speed feedback is recorded by interrupt mechanism.

1. Carefully wire the L298N motor driver, 9V power supply, and the DC
   motor to the MCU board. Wire the circuit according to the new
   circuit schematic on blackboard. If the motor is wired in a
   reversed way, the encoder will be damaged. Remember to share
   common ground. (10%)
2. Configure a PWM channel to drive the DC motor. Make sure the one
   you choose has an output on the motor PWM pin. Choose your timer
   prescaling factor, period, and duty cycle. (10%)
3. Configure EXTI on the motor encoder input pin (External Interrupt
   Mode with Rising edge trigger detection) with interrupt enabled,
   to collect encoder reading with an interrupt subroutine. Convert
   to rotations per second/minute and display on Tera Term. (10%)
4. Use the ring buffer and the parser, program a command to hardcode
   2 different duty cycles of the PWM signal. When input "DUTY100",
   the PWM duty cycle is set to 100% and the motor shaft is spinning
   at full speed. When input "DUTY50", the motor shaft spins at 50%
   speed, and so on. (30%)
5. When this "DUTY" command has been received, show RPM on Tera Term
   with 1Hz update rate. (20%)
6. Use the ring buffer and the parser, program a command to stop the
   motor and RPM reporting. For example "HALT". (20%)
